How the tool learns — and how it deliberately doesn't

The AI system doesn't retrain itself. It recalls your firm's past good searches instantly, and improves structurally through reviewed weekly updates — every change approved by a person before it goes live.

What is live today

Consultants can save a search they judge good and recall it later by its ID — fully editable. The saving is the quality filter: only searches a consultant chose to keep enter the firm's memory. What the tool does not do yet: use those saved searches to make the next generation better. Each output today is as good as the first day's — no more, no less.

How improvement will work — three steps, one human gate

1
Remember

Every generated search, every save, and quick thumbs-up / thumbs-down taps are logged automatically. No extra work for consultants.

2
Reference

When a consultant runs a new search, the system pulls up Ishwa's most similar past good searches and uses them as worked examples — so output reflects how your team actually searches.

3
Review

Once a week, the log is summarised into a short digest with proposed refinements. A person reviews and approves before anything changes. Nothing shifts silently.

The approval step is a feature, not a limitation: it means Ishwa can always say, in one sentence, how its tool behaves and why.
